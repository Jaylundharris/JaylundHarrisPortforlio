NFL Team Performance Analytics — Case Study

Role: Sports Data Analyst (Personal Project)

Tools: Python, Pandas, SQL, SQLite, Power BI

Question

The goal of this project was to understand long-term NFL team performance through win rates and scoring trends. I wanted to answer questions like: How do home and away win rates differ by team? Which teams historically overperform as favorites or underdogs? Are there clear patterns in margin of victory over time?

Data & Modeling

I worked with a historical NFL dataset containing game-level results by team and season. Using Python and Pandas, I cleaned and reshaped the data to a team–season structure and then loaded it into a SQLite database for querying.

The main fields included:

  • Team, opponent, season, and game location (home/away/neutral)
  • Final scores and margin of victory
  • Favorite/underdog indicator and closing spread
  • Game result (win/loss/push)

Analysis & Metrics

Using SQL and Python, I engineered metrics at the team level to evaluate historical performance:

  • Total games played by team and season
  • Home win rate vs. away win rate
  • Favorite win rate and underdog win rate
  • Average and total margin of victory
  • Seasonal trends in home/away performance over time

These metrics were then brought into Power BI to build interactive dashboards with slicers for team and season.

Key Insights

  • Several teams exhibited strong long-term home-field advantage, with home win rates significantly higher than away win rates, while others were more balanced or even performed better on the road.
  • Favorite and underdog win rates varied meaningfully by franchise, highlighting teams that historically outperformed expectations as underdogs.
  • Margin-of-victory trends over time showed periods of dominance and decline for specific teams, helping contextualize how a franchise’s performance has changed across eras.

Outcome

This project demonstrates my ability to clean and model sports data, engineer performance metrics, and build dashboards that surface meaningful trends for decision-making. The same approach can apply to betting strategy, team analysis, or internal reporting for sports organizations.
